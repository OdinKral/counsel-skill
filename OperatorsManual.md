# Council — Operator's Manual

*How to be a good host for your thinking partners.*

---

## The Core Idea

You have 5 coworkers with distinct expertise. You don't invite all 5 to every meeting. You pick the right people for the problem, give them good context, let them work, and afterwards you check whether the meeting was worth having.

That's it. Everything else is just making that easy.

---

## Before the Session: Framing

The single most important thing you do is **frame the question well**. A good frame has:

1. **The real question** — not the surface question. "Should we add collapsible panels?" is surface. "How do we get more canvas space for field work without losing tool access?" is real.

2. **What you already know** — don't make the team rediscover your existing thinking. "I've already ruled out hiding the toolbar because..." saves everyone time.

3. **What kind of help you need** — are you stuck? Deciding between options? Stress-testing a plan? Exploring open space? This determines who to invite.

### Quick Frame Template (optional, not required)

```
Topic: [the real question]
Context: [what you already know, 2-3 sentences]
Help needed: [stuck / deciding / stress-testing / exploring / sequencing]
```

---

## Picking the Team

You can pick explicitly, or ask for a Pre-Brief and let the Oracle recommend.

### When to use each mode

**Pipe (1 voice)** — You know exactly what kind of thinking you need.
- "I need scenarios" → Oracle
- "Is this plan solid?" → Critic
- "How do I build this?" → Engineer
- "I'm stuck" → Coach
- "How do we sequence this?" → Strategist
- "Am I overcomplicating?" → Sage

**Flex Team (2-3 voices)** — The problem has multiple dimensions but you don't need everyone.
- Building something with trade-offs → Engineer + Oracle
- Decision with risk → Critic + Oracle
- Stuck on something strategic → Coach + Strategist
- Complexity check → Critic + Sage

**Council (4 voices)** — Open exploration. You're not sure what you don't know.

**War Council (all voices)** — The decision is irreversible, high-stakes, or emotionally charged.

**Pre-Brief first** — You're not sure which mode to use. Let the Oracle scan and recommend.

### The "Coworker" Test

Ask: "If this were a real team, who would I pull into a room for this?" That's your flex team. If the answer is "everybody," it's a council. If the answer is "just Sarah from engineering," it's a pipe.

## During the Session: Hosting

**You're the decision-maker, not a spectator.** The voices advise; you decide.

### Good hosting habits

- **Redirect if off-track.** "That's interesting but not what I need — focus on the sequencing question."
- **Push back on hedging.** "You're giving me three maybes. Which one would you bet on?"
- **Add context mid-session.** "Actually, I forgot to mention — we tried that last month and it didn't work because..."
- **Request another voice.** "This is sounding like the Strategist should weigh in. Bring them in."
- **Kill a tangent.** "Let's park that and come back to the core question."

### When to interrupt

- The voices are rehashing what you already know
- A voice is hedging when you need a clear position
- You realize the framing was wrong — reframe and restart
- You already have your answer — don't wait for the synthesis

### When to let it run

- You're hearing something you didn't expect
- Two voices disagree and the tension is productive
- The second-order effects reveal something you missed

## After the Session: The Retro

**Within 48 hours**, fill in the three retro questions at the bottom of the session output:

```
## Session Value (fill within 48 hours)

- Action taken: [yes/no]
- Insight I wouldn't have reached alone: [yes/no]
- Right team for this question: [yes/no] (who was missing / who wasn't needed?)
```

**Why this matters:** These three questions are the utility function for the entire system. Without them, you're running meetings with no way to know if they're worth having. With them, the Sage can analyze patterns over time:

- Which team compositions produce the most action?
- Which problem types benefit from more voices vs. fewer?
- Are you invoking full councils when a pipe would do?
- Are sessions generating insights or just confirming what you already thought?

### Filling it out well

- **Action taken** = Did you actually DO something within 48 hours because of this session? Not "plan to do" — actually did.
- **Insight** = Did a voice surface something you genuinely hadn't considered? If every session is "yes," you might be under-thinking before the session. If every session is "no," the sessions might be theater.
- **Right team** = In hindsight, was anyone unnecessary? Was someone missing? "The Critic wasn't needed — there was no real risk here" or "Should have had the Strategist — we missed the sequencing angle."

---

## The Meta-Loop

Every few weeks (or during a Meta Review), the Sage looks at the retro data across sessions. Over time, patterns emerge:

- "Oracle + Engineer flex teams have 90% action rate for build questions"
- "Full councils have 40% action rate — most council topics were really pipe questions"
- "The Critic is overused on low-stakes decisions"

These patterns improve the Pre-Brief's team recommendations. The system learns which coworkers to invite to which meetings.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Always using full council | Start with pipe or flex team. Escalate if needed. |
| Never filling out retros | Set a reminder. No retro data = no learning. |
| Vague framing | Use the Quick Frame template until it's natural. |
| Ignoring dissent | If a voice disagrees with the conclusion, that's signal. Sit with it. |
| Running sessions when you already know the answer | If you already decided, just do it. Sessions are for genuine uncertainty. |
| Not pushing back | You're the host. Redirect, interrupt, reframe. The voices serve you. |
| Over-scheduling | The guardrail: 5+ sessions without measurable action = stop and execute. |

---

## Quick Start

**First session:** Try a pipe. Pick the voice that matches your question. See how it feels.

**Second session:** Try a flex team with 2 voices. Notice how they interact differently than a full council.

**Third session:** Run a Pre-Brief before a session. See if the Oracle's team recommendation matches your instinct.

**After 5-6 sessions:** Fill in the retros. Run a Meta Review. Let the data tell you what's working.

---

*The best meeting is the one you didn't need to have. The second best is the one with exactly the right people in the room.*
